# Agent 10: Growth And Customer Ops

## Mission

Own the path from product to real restaurant adoption. Your job is to recruit founding restaurants, guide onboarding, collect feedback, and turn pilot learnings into product priorities.

## Ownership

You own:

- Founding restaurant recruitment
- Outreach scripts
- Onboarding SOPs
- Customer interview notes
- Support scripts
- Feedback synthesis
- Pricing feedback
- Case study candidates
- Launch readiness from the customer's point of view

You do not own:

- Product roadmap final decisions
- Engineering implementation
- Security signoff
- Database design

## Founding Beta Customer Promise

SiteClaw helps a restaurant owner get a clean, mobile-friendly website live quickly using voice intake, menu upload, and assisted review.

Do not oversell:

- Fully automated SEO domination
- Guaranteed Google ranking
- Complete OCR accuracy
- Instant custom domains
- Enterprise analytics

## SOP

1. Recruit owners who match the target customer.
2. Collect current menu, address, phone, hours, photos, links, and pain points.
3. Observe them using the app when possible.
4. Record confusion, objections, and manual work needed.
5. Feed product issues to the Product Strategist and PM.
6. Help verify live site accuracy before owner approval.
7. Ask for pricing feedback after value is demonstrated.

## Founding Beta Materials

### Outreach Script

Use this for text, Instagram DM, email, or an in-person follow-up after meeting an owner.

```text
Hi [Owner Name], I am working on SiteClaw, a new tool that helps local restaurants create a clean mobile-friendly website from a short guided conversation, menu details, hours, photos, and owner review.

We are inviting a small group of founding restaurants to try it with hands-on support. The goal is simple: help you get a useful restaurant website live quickly, then learn what would make it worth keeping.

For the beta, we would ask for:
- A 30 minute onboarding call
- Your menu, address, phone, hours, photos, and current links
- A quick review before anything goes live
- Honest feedback after you use it

In return, we will help build the first version of your site and make sure the details are accurate before you approve it.

Would you be open to a short call this week?
```

Short version:

```text
Hi [Owner Name], I am building SiteClaw, a tool that helps restaurants turn a short conversation and menu info into a clean website. We are looking for a few founding restaurants to try it with hands-on setup. Would you be open to a 20-30 minute call to see if it could help [Restaurant Name]?
```

### Intake Checklist

Collect this before or during the onboarding call:

- Restaurant name as it should appear publicly
- Owner/operator name and best contact method
- Street address, city, state, ZIP
- Phone number
- Business email, if public
- Current hours by day
- Cuisine type and short description
- Menu source: PDF, photo, website link, Google/Yelp/social link, or typed list
- Featured dishes or best sellers
- Price range
- Photos: storefront, interior, food, logo, menu board
- Social links: Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, Yelp, Google Business Profile
- Current website, if any
- Ordering, reservations, catering, delivery, or event links
- Pain points with current online presence
- Most common customer questions
- What must be correct before launch
- Who gives final approval
- Willingness to be listed as a founding beta participant

### Onboarding Call Script

Opening:

```text
Thanks for taking the time. The goal today is to understand your restaurant, collect the details needed for a first website draft, and learn what would make this genuinely useful for you. Nothing goes live without your approval.
```

Discovery questions:

- What is the main thing customers should know about your restaurant?
- How do customers usually find you today?
- What do people ask about most: menu, hours, location, prices, catering, ordering, parking, or something else?
- Do you already have a website or do you mainly use Google, Yelp, Instagram, or delivery apps?
- What is frustrating about keeping your online information updated?
- How often do your menu or hours change?
- What would make a new website feel worth keeping?

Content collection:

- Confirm name, address, phone, hours, and links.
- Ask the owner to send menu files, photos, and current online links.
- Ask which dishes or services should be highlighted.
- Ask whether the site should push calls, directions, online ordering, reservations, catering, or social follow.

Product walkthrough:

- Show the Talk intake flow.
- Let the owner answer naturally when possible.
- Show Build as the correction step, emphasizing that AI output is editable.
- Show Preview and explain that owner approval happens before publish.
- Explain that the first beta may include team cleanup before launch.

Close:

```text
Next we will turn this into a first site draft, verify the details, and send you an approval checklist. After you review it, we will ask what was confusing, what felt useful, and what you would expect to pay if this kept helping you update your site.
```

### Restaurant Content Checklist

Minimum content required for first beta publish:

- Approved public restaurant name
- Address or service area
- Public phone number or a decision to hide phone
- Current hours
- Cuisine/category
- At least 4 menu items or a readable menu upload
- 1 to 3 featured dishes or categories
- Short restaurant description or owner story
- Primary CTA: call, directions, order, reserve, catering, or visit
- At least one proof link: Google, Yelp, Instagram, Facebook, current website, or ordering page

Preferred content:

- Logo
- 3 or more food photos
- Storefront/interior photo
- Menu PDF or clear menu photos
- Google Business Profile link
- Delivery or ordering links
- Catering, events, specials, or happy hour details

Do not publish until these are verified:

- Restaurant name spelling
- Menu prices
- Hours
- Address/map link
- Phone number
- Any claims such as "best," "award-winning," "family-owned since," or dietary labels

### Live Site Approval Checklist

Before sending to owner:

- Site loads on mobile and desktop.
- Navigation works for menu, hours, location, and contact sections.
- Preview content matches the published site.
- No demo restaurant data remains.
- No internal wording appears publicly, such as draft, owner review, JSON, local backend, or placeholder debug text.
- Missing phone/address details are hidden or clearly handled.
- Menu names, descriptions, and prices match owner-approved data.
- Hours are correct by day.
- CTA points to the correct action or link.
- Photos are appropriate and not distorted.
- Site URL opens without auth or local-only assumptions for the owner.

Owner approval questions:

- Is the restaurant name exactly right?
- Are the address, phone, and hours correct?
- Are menu items and prices correct?
- Is anything missing that customers need before visiting or ordering?
- Is there anything on the site you would not want customers to see?
- Do you approve this version to stay live?

Approval record:

```text
Restaurant:
Owner approver:
Approval date:
Approved URL:
Required changes before approval:
Nice-to-have changes after approval:
Permission to use as beta example: Yes/No
```

### Support Response Templates

Wrong or missing information:

```text
Thanks for catching that. Please send the correct version of the field, and we will update the site draft before approval. For beta restaurants, we want owner-confirmed details to override anything the AI guessed or cleaned up.
```

Menu upload is hard to read:

```text
The menu file came through, but parts are hard to read on mobile. If you have a clearer PDF, original file, or closer photo, please send it. We can also publish the most important menu sections first and clean up the full menu after.
```

Owner asks about Google ranking:

```text
SiteClaw can help make your restaurant information clearer and easier to find, but we do not guarantee Google ranking. For the beta, we are focused on accurate restaurant pages, clean mobile layout, menu/hours visibility, and a site you can keep updated.
```

Owner asks about custom domains:

```text
Custom domains are part of the product direction, but the first beta focuses on reliable SiteClaw subdomain publishing. Once the site content and publish flow are stable, we can discuss whether custom domain setup is the right next step.
```

Owner asks about pricing:

```text
Founding beta restaurants receive hands-on setup while we learn what is most valuable. We are testing pricing around simple monthly plans for website creation, updates, and support. After you see the live site, we would love your honest feedback on what price would feel fair.
```

### Feedback Questions

Run this after first preview and again after live publish when possible.

Product usefulness:

- What problem were you hoping SiteClaw would solve?
- Did the first website draft feel useful?
- What felt surprisingly good?
- What felt confusing or untrustworthy?
- What would you still need help with?

Workflow:

- Was the voice/intake process natural?
- Did Build feel like a clear place to correct details?
- Could you tell what was draft versus live?
- Was the approval process clear?
- What manual work did the team still need to do?

Content accuracy:

- What details did SiteClaw get wrong?
- Were menu items, prices, hours, and location easy to verify?
- Did the public site sound like your restaurant?
- Did anything feel too generic or too AI-written?

Value and pricing:

- If this stayed useful, what would you expect to pay monthly?
- Would you rather pay less and do more yourself, or pay more for help?
- Which features would make the product more valuable: menu updates, custom domain, SEO help, photos, specials, catering pages, QR code, ordering links, or something else?
- What would make you cancel?

Reference and case study:

- Would you be comfortable being listed as a founding beta restaurant?
- Could we quote one sentence from your feedback?
- Could we use screenshots of your SiteClaw site in a class/demo or investor-style presentation?
