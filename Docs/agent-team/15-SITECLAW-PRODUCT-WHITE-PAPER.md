# SiteClaw Product White Paper

## Thesis

Local restaurant websites are still harder than they should be. Website builders ask owners to think like designers, marketers, developers, and SEO specialists. Agencies are slow and expensive. Social media pages are not a real home base. Menus change often, hours shift, owners are busy, and the website becomes stale.

SiteClaw turns the owner into the source of truth and lets AI handle the translation layer.

## The Opportunity

Restaurants need:

- A professional public website
- Current menu and prices
- Accurate hours
- Address, phone, and directions
- Search visibility basics
- Easy updates

Owners often have:

- A physical menu
- Photos
- Social links
- A story
- Local knowledge
- No extra time

The gap is not lack of information. The gap is converting rough owner input into clean, useful digital presence.

## The Product Insight

The best interface for many owners is not a blank form or design editor. It is conversation.

SiteClaw should let an owner say:

> "The restaurant is Plata Catering. We do Venezuelan food in San Diego. We are open 11 to 3, closed for an hour, then 4 to 8. The atmosphere, hospitality, and smell in the air are what make it special."

And produce:

- Clean business profile
- Structured hours
- Website copy
- Featured dishes
- Previewable site
- Live URL

## Why Restaurants First

Restaurants are a strong first market because:

- Every restaurant needs menu, hours, address, phone, and directions.
- Many restaurants have bad or missing websites.
- Menus and hours change often.
- Owners value speed and simplicity.
- Visual quality matters, but sites can be template-driven.
- Local SEO basics are understandable and valuable.

## Product Positioning

SiteClaw is not "another website builder."

SiteClaw is:

- A restaurant website assistant
- A voice-first onboarding tool
- A menu and local visibility system
- A publish-and-update workflow

Simple positioning:

> Talk to SiteClaw. Upload your menu. Launch your restaurant website.

## Strategic Wedge

Start with assisted self-serve.

This avoids two traps:

- Building a giant SaaS platform before knowing what restaurants pay for
- Becoming a pure agency with no scalable product

The founding beta should sit in the middle: owners use the app, the team observes and assists, and the product learns from every manual step.

## Defensibility

Early defensibility comes from:

- Restaurant-specific intake
- Voice cleanup and structured extraction
- Menu handling
- Local SEO workflow
- Publish/update simplicity
- Dataset of real owner speech patterns and corrections
- Templates tuned for restaurant archetypes

Longer-term defensibility comes from:

- Better extraction evals
- Restaurant-specific design patterns
- Publishing reliability
- Customer success playbooks
- Distribution partnerships with local business groups, POS consultants, and restaurant services

## Product Principles

- The owner should not need web vocabulary.
- AI should clean up speech, not merely transcribe it.
- Every AI result must be editable.
- Publishing should feel safe and reversible.
- Uploaded menus should be useful immediately.
- Preview and live site must match.
- Do not make fake SEO promises.

## Business Model

Initial model:

- Founding beta customers
- Manual or lightly assisted billing
- Plan gates visible in product

Likely plans:

- Starter: basic site, voice build, menu upload, SiteClaw subdomain
- Growth: custom domain, more update support, Growth Toolkit beta
- Pro: priority support, advanced visibility features, multi-location path

Pricing should be validated with real owners after they see a live site.

## Near-Term Roadmap

1. Production foundation
2. AI cleanup and extraction reliability
3. Live Cloudflare publishing
4. Founding restaurant onboarding
5. Paid early access
6. Stripe and plan enforcement
7. Custom domains
8. Stronger menu extraction
9. Self-serve V1

## Main Risks

- Overbuilding before customer validation
- AI field extraction damaging trust
- App UX becoming too complicated
- Menu upload not being readable enough
- Publishing infrastructure taking too long
- Weak customer acquisition

## Conclusion

SiteClaw should become the fastest path from restaurant owner knowledge to a live, useful restaurant website. The company should win first by being specific, practical, and owner-friendly. The product should not ask restaurant owners to become web builders. It should make the website from the way they already talk about their business.
